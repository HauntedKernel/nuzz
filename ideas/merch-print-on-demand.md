# Merch via print-on-demand (POD)

**Short answer: yes — exactly what you described already exists.** It's called **print-on-demand /
dropshipping**: you upload art, the service prints + ships each item *only when someone orders*, takes
the base cost + their fee, and you keep the margin you set. No inventory, no upfront cost, no packing
boxes. They get their cut, you get the rest.

## Read this first (the real constraints)
- **You need art.** Nuzz is drawn in code on a canvas — merch needs real illustrations (PNG/vector) of
  the pets. **That's the actual prerequisite**, before any platform.
- **Margins are thin** and it mostly pays once you have an *engaged* audience — tie this to the
  retention numbers, not raw traffic.
- **Product range varies:** shirts / stickers / mugs / posters are easy everywhere; **hats** are
  common; **keychains / enamel pins / acrylic charms** are more specialized (fewer POD providers).

## Options, by effort
- **Lowest effort — marketplaces (Redbubble, TeePublic):** upload art, they host the store + do
  everything, you get a royalty. Zero setup. Trade-off: low margin, they own the customer. Good to
  *validate* demand cheaply.
- **Creator storefront (Spring, Fourthwall):** they host a free store page, handle POD + payments,
  take a cut. No Shopify needed. Good middle ground for a solo build-in-public team.
- **Best margin/control (Printful or Printify + a simple store):** highest quality + margin, integrate
  into Shopify/Etsy or a static page. Printify = more product variety + cheaper providers (some do
  keychains/pins); Printful = most consistent quality; Gelato = strong international.
- **Stickers (Sticker Mule / StickerApp):** cheapest, cutest, most impulse-buy, extremely on-brand for
  cute animals. Small batches rather than strict per-order, but cheap to start.
- **Acrylic keychains / charms / pins (Vograce):** the artist go-to; low minimums, made-to-order-ish.

## Suggested path for Nuzz
1. **Get ONE real illustration** of a pet (chick / panda / the dog). Nothing else matters until this.
2. **Start with stickers** (Sticker Mule) — lowest risk, validates "will people buy Nuzz stuff."
3. **Add a Spring or Printful storefront** for shirts/hats once stickers prove demand.
4. **Link it from the end-card + landing strip**, same as the giveaway CTA and the tip jar.
