# Clean the Oil off the Birds

**One-liner:** An oil-spill rescue — gently scrub the oil off seabirds until their feathers are clean.

**Core verb:** swipe-to-clean — closest to the dog's petting/scrubbing drag.

**Sketch:**
- Oil-slicked birds arrive (sad, sticky, dark). Swipe back and forth across a bird to wipe the oil
  away; a "clean" meter fills as you scrub. A fully clean bird brightens up and flaps off happily.
- The heaviest-oiled patches need the most scrubbing (sweet-spot style). Maybe pick up a sponge/soap
  (like the nest's worm) for extra scrubbing power and a touch of intention.
- More birds arrive over time; timed round. Ramp = more birds / heavier oil.
- Consequence: a **wildlife volunteer** verdict — birds you fully clean fly free; ones left oily are
  "still in care." Gentle and hopeful; no death.

**Brand fit:** VERY on-brand — wholesome, caring, quietly meaningful (real wildlife-rescue vibe), and
it reuses the cozy scrub mechanic. Possibly the most "Nuzz" idea of all.

**Open questions:**
- Finger-only scrub, or a pick-up-a-sponge tool (adds intention like the worm)?
- Stakes, or generous/meditative like petting? (Lean: generous — a calm care level; difficulty is
  volume + time, not failure.)
- Natural fit for a real-world charity tie-in someday — great build-in-public goodwill.
